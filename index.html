About Me

Hi! I'm Christina, a ChBE student at Georgia Tech with a passion for using data and process modeling to solve complex engineering problems. My current research focuses on developing an inverse solver for a high-fidelity mechanochemical mill model (50+ ODEs) using Pyomo, IPOPT, and parameter estimation techniques. I have also contributed to biomedical device research (NIH-funded preterm birth prevention studies), launched a gluten- and nut-free cookie mix business, and worked as a part-time data analytics engineer for a carbon-capture startup.

I am equally comfortable in technical, entrepreneurial, and cross-functional environments. My skillset includes process simulation, optimization, data analysis, and computational modeling, with proficiency in Python, Pyomo, MATLAB, and scientific communication. I’m especially interested in roles at the intersection of chemical engineering, data science, and product innovation (particularly in food, biotech, and sustainable manufacturing).
